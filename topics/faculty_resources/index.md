## Jupyter Notebooks

If you want your studnets to work in Jupyter Notebooks in your course, there are
at least two options:

* JupyterHub, provided through L&S IT
* Google CoLab, provides as part of the UCSB Google Drive subscription

### Requesting a JupyterHub site

To request a JupyterHub site as a faculty member, use this link: 
* <https://jhub-request.lsit.ucsb.edu>

### Using Google Colab at UCSB

For Google Colab, access Google Drive after logging in with your UCSBNetId.

Click the `+ New` button, and you should see something like the screenshot below.
* Use the `More` option at the bottom of the menu, and then look in the additional options.
* If you already see `Google Colaboratory`, then you have Google Colab installed already.
* If not, go to the bottom option that says `+ Connect More Apps`. Search for "Colaboratory" and go through the steps to install it.  You will need to instruct your students to do this also.
